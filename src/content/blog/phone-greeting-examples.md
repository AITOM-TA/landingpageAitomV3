Your **phone greeting** is the first voice a customer hears. In ten seconds, it sets the tone: polished or sloppy, reassuring or rushed. Below are more than 15 ready-to-copy professional phone greeting examples, organized by industry and by situation, plus the writing rules that separate a great greeting from a forgettable one, and why even the best scripted message quickly shows its limits.

## The rules of a great phone greeting

An effective greeting comes down to four principles:

- **Short**: 10 to 15 seconds max. Any longer and people hang up.
- **Identifiable**: your company name in the very first sentence.
- **Useful**: one actionable detail (hours, an alternative, a callback window).
- **Warm**: a calm, smiling tone. It comes through, even on voicemail.

## Examples for a business (general purpose)

> "Hi, and thanks for calling [Company]. We're not available to take your call right now. Please leave your name, number, and the reason for your call, and we'll get back to you the same day. Thanks, and talk soon."

> "You've reached [Company]. Our offices are open Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. Outside those hours, please leave a message or email us at [email]."

## Examples for a practice (medical, legal)

> "Thank you for calling [Name] Practice. To request an appointment, please leave your name and number after the tone and we'll call you right back. If this is an emergency, please dial [phone number]."

> "You've reached [Practice Name]. Our office is open [hours]. For prescription refills, leave your name, date of birth, and pharmacy. For anything else, leave your name and number and we'll call you back. If this is a medical emergency, hang up and dial 911."

> "You've reached the law offices of [Name]. Our hours are [hours]. Please leave your contact information and the reason for your call, and we'll get back to you as soon as possible."

> "Thank you for calling the law offices of [Name]. If you're a current client, please leave your name and case reference. If you're calling about a new matter, briefly describe it and we'll call you back within one business day."

## Examples for a contractor / on-call service

> "Hi, this is [Name] with [Company]. I'm out on a job right now. Leave me a message with your address and a quick description of the problem, and I'll call you back as soon as I can."

> "You've reached [Company], your [trade] repair service. If this is an emergency, say so in your message: I handle emergencies first."

> "Thank you for calling [Company] Plumbing and Heating. All of our technicians are out on jobs. Leave your name, address, and what's going on, and we'll call you back before [time]. For after-hours emergencies, call [number]."

## Examples for a real estate agency

> "Thank you for calling [Agency]. Our agents are with clients right now. Leave your name, number, and the property address you're calling about, and we'll get back to you today."

> "You've reached [Agency]. Calling about a listing? Leave the address or reference number and your phone number. Thinking of selling? Say so in your message and we'll call you back to set up a free valuation."

## Examples for a restaurant

> "Thank you for calling [Restaurant]. We're open [days], [hours]. For reservations, leave your name, number, party size, and preferred time, and we'll confirm by text. You can also book online at [website]."

> "You've reached [Restaurant]. We're in the middle of service and can't take your call. For tonight, leave your name and party size. For private events, email [email]."

## Short examples (simple voicemail)

> "[Company], hi. Leave a message and we'll call you back."

> "You've reached [Company]. Leave a message after the tone and we'll call you back today. Thanks!"

## Bilingual example (international clientele)

> "Hi, you've reached [Company]. / Hola, se ha comunicado con [Company]. Please leave a message / por favor deje un mensaje."

## Greetings for every situation

### During business hours (live answer)

> "Good morning, [Company], this is [Name]. How can I help you?"

The greeting is only the opening line; what happens next matters just as much. Our guide to [professional call answering](/en/blog/professional-call-answering) covers the rest of the call.

### Holiday closure

> "Thank you for calling [Company]. Our office is closed for [holiday] and will reopen [date] at [time]. Leave a message and we'll get back to you when we return."

Record it before you leave, and set a reminder to remove it when you're back.

### Hold message

> "Thank you for holding. Your call will be answered in order. If you'd rather not wait, leave a message after the tone and we'll call you back before the end of the day."

Never leave pure silence on hold: callers assume the line dropped and hang up.

### Overflow (all lines busy)

> "All of our lines are busy at the moment. Please stay on the line, or press 1 to leave a message and keep your place for a callback."

### After-hours voicemail

> "You've reached [Company] outside our business hours. We're open [hours]. Leave your name, number, and the reason for your call, and we'll get back to you first thing in the morning."

## How to write a professional phone greeting: six tips

1. **Write for the ear, not the eye.** Short words, short sentences. Read it aloud before recording; if you stumble, rewrite.
2. **One action per message.** Leave a message, or call another number, or book online. Not all three.
3. **Front-load the essentials.** Company name and the key information in the first five seconds.
4. **Only promise what you keep.** "We'll call you back today" is powerful, but only if it's true.
5. **Keep it current.** Re-record when hours change, and retire seasonal messages on time. A holiday greeting in February says more than it should.
6. **Record it well.** Quiet room, several takes, and smile while speaking: it changes the sound of your voice.

### Mistakes to avoid

- "Your call is important to us": the phrase everyone hears as its opposite.
- Listing every department and menu option before saying anything useful.
- Jokes and sound effects that age badly.
- Background noise, or a greeting recorded in one rushed take.
- An outdated message: wrong hours cost you calls every single day.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long should a professional phone greeting be?

Aim for 10 to 15 seconds, roughly 30 to 40 words. Callers decide within the first few seconds whether to stay on the line, so put your company name and the one thing you want them to do right up front.

### Should I record the greeting myself or hire a voice?

For a small business, your own voice usually works better: it's authentic and matches who callers will actually reach. Hire a voice professional if your recording environment is noisy or if the greeting sits on a main line with heavy volume. Either way, script it first and do several takes.

### How often should I update my phone greeting?

Review it every quarter, and immediately after any change in hours, address, or team. Seasonal messages (holidays, summer closures) need a removal date the moment you record them. An outdated greeting quietly misroutes callers every day.

### What should an after-hours greeting always include?

Four things: your company name, your actual business hours, what to leave in the message (name, number, reason), and when the caller will hear back. If some calls can't wait until morning, add an emergency option with a real number behind it.
